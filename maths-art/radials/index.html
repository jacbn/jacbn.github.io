Radial Circles

What's going on?

Here we have a super simple setup; there's a starting circle (say \(C_0\)), and a pointer that rotates around this at a constant speed while drawing a path behind it. The path of the pointer does nothing interesting yet; it just follows the circle. But, we can add a second circle (say \(C_1\)) in place of the pointer, with some non-zero radius, and have the pointer rotate around this circle instead.

Click the "Add Circle" button to see what happens!

Can we change the shapes that are made?

Sure! The circles' relative radii and rotation speeds both change the shapes produced. In theory, any continuous curve can be produced given enough circles*. There's a number of nice examples in the examples tab, but it's great fun to experiment (make sure to try negative speeds)!

*I'm not sure this JS simulation will quite have the precision for works of art, though!

What is this, mathematically?

The shapes made between two circles are called epicycloids, and when there are multiple circles it's called a nested epicycloid.


Here's a bonus challenge I stumbled over while coding this: how many rotations will each circle do around its parent circle before everything returns to its original position?

Answer Each circle \(i\) will go through \(\big\lvert \frac{speeds_i}{\text{hcf(}speeds\text{)}} \big\rvert\) rotations!
